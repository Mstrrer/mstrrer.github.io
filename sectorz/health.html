The Basics

The health system is quite easy to understand. First thing you should know, is that every injury has specific properties.
These properties define how serious a wound is, what is affected by the severity of the wound, and how likely you are gonna die. Injuries is a loose term but is still used for any effect or condition that the player is experiencing for simplicity.

Another big thing to know is that injuries either worsen or get better when left alone (with treatment or not, depends on wound). Most injuries will affect your overall vitality. Think of this as your health points, though just overly complicated for the sake of realism.

Basic Properties

  • The location of an injury indicates where it is located, as different locations affect different things. For example, if you got a cut in your hand, you'd likely be fine and it would heal up fast and easy. Though, if you got a cut in your eye, you might not be able to see for a while. Now, understand that some injuries are non location-specific, and is affecting the whole body.

  • The severity or strength of an injury indicates how screwed you are, every wound has a specific line of effects that change depending on the severity. Though, this severity can decay or worsen depending on the injury.

  • As stated like a second ago, the rate of decay says how much a condition worsens or decays in a specific amount of time. If it gets worse through time, maybe you should patch that up before you lose too much blood.

  • You may ask, how do you determine if someone is unconscious... Well that answer is pain, it uses the same system as RimWorld does, where you have a certain pain threshold that determines when you fall. Most wounds give you a good amount of pain. And your pain threshold is increased by 10 pain units for every vitality point above 0 (with a base pain threshold of 80).

  • Some injuries will be mostly hidden to the players (like blood loss), because of this, most wounds/injuries are given a symptoms (aka visual or feeling effects) that can help you determine what this hidden wound is. Diagnosing is done by an Intelligence check (described below), high difficulty diagnosis can result is misdiagnosing!

As mentioned above, diagnosing is decided by an Intelligence check. Though not just diagnosing is dictated by this, medical procedures is also dictated by this. Luckily, you don't have to do it every time (unless you want to, in case you believe you've misdiagnosed yourself) as you remember things (amazing, never knew that). These memories are stored in the other section of your character sheet. Remember, a lot of things are common knowledge, and are by default already remembered (unless you have amnesiac detriment).
