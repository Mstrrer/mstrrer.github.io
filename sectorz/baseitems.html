Weapons

Canada is known for hunting, and you'll often find hunting rifles around, but only sometimes something for self defense. Why is that? Well, due to the restrictions of guns in Canada, not many civilians have as much guns as their redneck neighbors in the south. Though, you might find a load of good stuff in specific places.

But woah, don't just be mindlessly swinging your stuff! Blunt and sharp weapons have chances to break on failed checks, rendering their damage miniscule, but still able to be used. And if a gun is just an unreliable gun or dirty or bloody (which usually comes from bashing a zombie's head in and it splats onto your gun), you have a chance for it to jam every time you shoot, this chance is low, but still a possibility.

Weapon Types

There are a few different weapon types that revolve around specific damage or intensity. Here's a list:

  • Firearms - Represents bigger, stronger hitting, firearms. Usually rifles, shotguns, submachine guns, and etc.

  • Smallarms - Represents smaller handguns, and more common, firearms.

  • Blunt - Blunt melee is a weapon that does damage with a bashing movement, and usually never involves cutting open skin. Very useful for knocking down zombies and others.

  • Sharp - Sharp melee is a weapon that does damage by cutting open skin, often inflicting more pain, and a slow killing bleed effect. Not reccomended for knock back and etc.

Another note is that almost every weapon can be used as a blunt weapon if you use it correctly, it just won't be effective as standard blunt weapons. Though, can come in handy if you run out of ammunition for your rifle.
